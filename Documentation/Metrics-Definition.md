# Metrics & Dimensions Definitions

## Key Metrics

### Total Sales
- **Definition:** The aggregate revenue generated from all completed sales transactions within the selected time period.
- **Why This Metric Exists:** Total Sales is the primary indicator of business scale and market demand. It answers the most fundamental question: *How much revenue is the business generating?*
- **Purpose It Serves:** Establishes a baseline for all other performance metrics such as profit, margin, and customer value.
- **Analytical Insight:** Enables comparison across regions, products, and industries to identify revenue drivers and concentration risks.
- **Value:** $2.30M

---

### Total Profit
- **Definition:** The net financial gain after subtracting all associated costs from total sales.
- **Why This Metric Exists:** Revenue alone does not indicate business health. Profit highlights whether sales activities are financially sustainable.
- **Purpose It Serves:** Helps stakeholders evaluate cost efficiency, pricing effectiveness, and overall financial performance.
- **Analytical Insight:** When analyzed alongside Total Sales, it reveals high-revenue but low-margin products or regions.
- **Value:** $286.40K

---

### Total Orders
- **Definition:** The total count of completed sales transactions.
- **Why This Metric Exists:** Order volume provides context to revenue figures and indicates customer purchasing behavior.
- **Purpose It Serves:** Differentiates between revenue driven by high transaction volume versus high deal size.
- **Analytical Insight:** Useful for identifying demand patterns, sales velocity, and operational workload.
- **Value:** 5,009

---

### Total Customers
- **Definition:** The number of unique customer accounts that have completed at least one transaction.
- **Why This Metric Exists:** Measures the breadth of the customer base rather than transactional activity.
- **Purpose It Serves:** Indicates market reach and customer acquisition effectiveness.
- **Analytical Insight:** When paired with revenue metrics, it helps distinguish between growth driven by new customers versus repeat purchases.
- **Dashboard Value:** 9,994

---

### Average Order Value (AOV)
- **Formula:** Total Sales รท Total Orders
- **Why This Metric Exists:** AOV captures the average revenue generated per transaction.
- **Purpose It Serves:** Helps assess pricing strategy, bundling effectiveness, and upselling performance.
- **Analytical Insight:** A declining AOV may indicate discounting pressure, while a rising AOV suggests stronger deal quality.
- **Value:** $458.61

---

### Revenue per Customer
- **Formula:** Total Sales รท Total Customers
- **Why This Metric Exists:** Measures the average monetary value contributed by each customer.
- **Purpose It Serves:** Evaluates customer quality rather than just customer count.
- **Analytical Insight:** Enables identification of high-value customer segments and comparison across products or regions.
- **Value:** $23.20K

---

### Profit Margin
- **Formula:** (Total Profit รท Total Sales) ร 100
- **Why This Metric Exists:** Profit Margin normalizes profitability relative to revenue, making performance comparable across segments.
- **Purpose It Serves:** Indicates pricing efficiency, cost control, and overall operational effectiveness.
- **Analytical Insight:** Highlights areas where revenue growth does not translate into proportional profit growth.
- **Value:** 12.45%

---

## Dimensions Used

### Region
- **Definition:** High-level geographical grouping (Americas, Europe & Middle East, Asia Pacific & Japan).
- **Purpose:** Enables regional performance comparison and market prioritization.
- **Analytical Insight:** Identifies growth regions, underperforming markets, and regional dependency risks.

---

### Country
- **Definition:** Country-level classification within each region.
- **Purpose:** Supports granular geographic analysis beyond regional summaries.
- **Analytical Insight:** Helps pinpoint country-specific opportunities and performance gaps.

---

### Industry
- **Definition:** Industry category associated with each customer.
- **Purpose:** Segments revenue and customers by business sector.
- **Analytical Insight:** Reveals industry dependence, diversification opportunities, and sector-specific performance trends.

---

### Product
- **Definition:** Individual product or solution sold (e.g., ContactMatcher, FinanceHub, Site Analytics).
- **Purpose:** Enables product-level performance and profitability analysis.
- **Analytical Insight:** Helps identify flagship products, underperformers, and candidates for pricing or cost optimization.
